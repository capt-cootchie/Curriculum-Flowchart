Pacing

Realistic-pace calendars are the default.

Each track is paced at 5-7 hours/week, the rate at which a working autodidact actually internalizes material rather than skimming. Math is 130 weeks (~2.5 years). Drawing is 95 weeks. Painting is 130 weeks. Comics/Animation is 100 weeks. Metamaterials is 90 weeks. Specialized physics tracks range from 24-50 weeks depending on depth.

At 10 hours/week total study time, run 1-2 tracks at a time. Three tracks adds 80-100% to each timeline. Four or more produces superficial progress on everything. The calendar files (.ics) live alongside this document — import only what you're actively running. See HOW_TO_USE_CURRICULUM_SYSTEM.md for the full activation logic.

The 90-Week Path (calendar)
Phase I · Weeks 1-28 — Textbook Foundations. Field history & Veselago; Engheta-Ziolkowski overview; Tretyakov homogenization theory; Marqués negative-index theory & experiment; Pendry transformation optics; Leonhardt geometric foundation; Maier plasmonics (Drude → SPPs → LSPR → enhancement); Cai-Shalaev optical metamaterials; Capasso metasurfaces; Cui coding metamaterials; acoustic & mechanical metamaterials.
Phase II · Weeks 29-58 — Primary Literature. Re-read foundational papers; Pendry group (1996 wire array → 1999 SRR → 2006 transformation optics); Smith experimental papers; Engheta optical nanoantennas; Alù scattering-cancellation cloaks; Capasso metasurfaces 2011→present; Shalaev optical negative-index; topological photonics (Joannopoulos/Soljačić); PT-symmetric photonics; time-varying metamaterials; specialization choice and deep reading.
Phase III · Weeks 59-90 — Simulation & Capstone. MEEP/COMSOL setup; verification against analytics; SRR & wire array simulations; full negative-index unit cell; cloak demonstration; plasmonic nanoparticle & antenna; metasurface elements & metalens design; perfect absorber; capstone project (8 weeks from concept to documentation).
Prerequisites assumed solid: Griffiths E&M, Hecht optics, Kittel solid-state. Plasma physics (Chen) and crystallography helpful for plasmonic and topological subareas. From cold start with no prerequisites, add 18-24 months of prerequisite work before week 1.

The 44-Week Path (calendar)
Phase I · Weeks 1-12 — Foundations. Needham geometric complex analysis; sinusoids → phasors; impedance & the four-quadrant complex plane; AC power & the P-Q plane. Both four-quadrant geometries with their distinct meanings.
Phase II · Weeks 13-26 — Steinmetz directly. Engineering Mathematics; Theory and Calculation of Alternating Current Phenomena (1916) — single-phase, polyphase, transformers, induction motors; non-sinusoidal waves & harmonics; transients.
Phase III · Weeks 27-34 — Heaviside. Nahin biography & Hunt's Maxwellians for context; Electromagnetic Theory vols 1-3; transmission lines & operational calculus; bridge to modern Laplace transforms.
Phase IV · Weeks 35-40 — Tesla-era primary sources. Tesla Colorado Springs Notes; Alexanderson papers; John Stone Stone; Aitken & Hughes for historical context.
Phase V · Weeks 41-44 — Contemporary recovery. Dollard's Four Quadrant Representation with foundations now in place; broader recovery community read critically; synthesis document.
Honest framing: Steinmetz & Heaviside are unambiguously legitimate recovery work — texts working engineers should still read. Tesla primary sources are limited but worth reading directly. Dollard's engineering recovery has real substance; his broader cosmological framing is harder to evaluate — engage critically. Track doesn't take a position. Total cost relying on free primary sources: under $100. End state: engineering literacy very few working engineers have.

Visual Reference

For daily reference while working. Color wheels, value scales, hatching, perspective, head proportions, gesture, anatomy.

Color · 12-Hue Wheel
The Standard Color Wheel
Twelve-hue wheel. Primary triad (R-Y-B) shown in center triangle. Complements are diametrically opposite. Split-complement uses the two hues flanking the direct complement. Analogous schemes use 3-5 adjacent hues.
Value · 9-Step Scale
Value Scale (Black to White)
Most beginners use only the middle three values. Master paintings push toward the extremes. Squint at any subject — what you can still see is its value structure.
Mark-making · Ink and Pencil
Hatching Vocabulary
Hatch density controls value; direction controls form. Cross-hatching darkens further. Contour hatch (curving with the form) makes ink read three-dimensional. Stippling is slow but unmatched for texture variation.
Perspective · 1, 2, and 3 Point
Vanishing Point Constructions
One-point for interiors and roads receding away. Two-point for street corners and buildings at ground level. Three-point for dramatic angles — VP3 above (worm's eye) or below (bird's eye) the page; verticals lean toward it.
Head Construction · Loomis Method
Loomis Head Proportions
Loomis ball-and-jaw method. Vertical centerline and horizontal feature lines (brow → nose → mouth) divide the face. Brow to chin in three equal parts. In any non-frontal view, the centerline and feature lines curve around the form — getting that curvature right is what makes the head three-dimensional.
Figure · Heads-Tall Proportions
Standard Figure Proportions
Average adult is 7.5 heads tall (Loomis). Heroic/comic-book proportions are 8 heads. Idealized fashion proportions are 8.5 heads. Key landmarks: nipples at 2 heads, navel at 3, crotch at 4, knees at 5.5-6, feet at 7.5-8.5.
Gesture · Force Lines
Mattesi Force Direction
Mattesi's Force approach: every gesture has a directional rhythm before any anatomy. C-curves carry weight in one direction. S-curves create contrapposto. Reverse-C suggests muscular tension. Find the force line first; anatomy follows.
Hand Construction · Bridgman Method
Hand as Wedge + Block of Fingers
Bridgman's method: hand = palm wedge + block of four fingers + opposing thumb (separate wedge). Each finger has three segments that curl at each knuckle. Middle finger length roughly equals palm length. Drill until automatic — hands are where most figures fail.
Composition · Templates
Compositional Frameworks
Four classical composition systems. Rule of thirds places subjects on intersections. Golden ratio (φ ≈ 1.618) guides the eye through a Fibonacci spiral. Dynamic symmetry uses diagonal armatures (Hambidge, Bouleau). Payne's masses organize the image as interlocking large shapes. Most accomplished compositions use more than one.
Watercolor · Wet / Dry Technique
Watercolor Wash Types
Flat wash: even tone. Graded wash: smooth dark-to-light transition. Wet-on-wet: soft edges, watercolor's signature look. Wet-on-dry: hard edges and control. Most paintings combine all four — wet-on-wet for atmosphere, wet-on-dry for foreground detail.
Oil Paint · Drying Times
Oil Color Drying Rates
Oil drying times vary dramatically by pigment. Earth colors dry in days; cadmiums and alizarin take a week+. "Fat over lean" rule: each layer should have more oil than the one beneath. Build paintings from fast-drying underpainting upward. Standard underpainting: raw umber or burnt sienna with solvent.
Animation · 12 Principles
Disney's 12 Principles of Animation
Even if you never animate, the 12 principles improve your comic and illustration work. Comics is animation with the in-betweens missing — the reader fills them in. Williams' Animator's Survival Kit is the bible; the principles originate with Thomas & Johnston's Illusion of Life. Panel timing is the equivalent of frame timing — bigger panels read slower.
Comics · McCloud Transitions
Six Panel Transitions
McCloud's transition taxonomy from Understanding Comics (1993). Western comics lean heavily on 2-4. Manga uses 5 (aspect-to-aspect) far more — it builds atmosphere by fragmenting a single moment across panels. Type 6 is rare and usually deliberate. Knowing the type tells you what work the gutter is doing.

This is a map, not an assignment.

Pick one or two from any category that calls to you, work through them, let what you learn guide the next pick. The cross-references between tracks matter more than the depth of any single track — the synthesis project lives in the crossings.
